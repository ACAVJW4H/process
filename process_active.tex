\subsubsection{Gate oxide growth}
The thickness of the gate oxide depends the capacity (slew rate) of the transistor.
The thinner the layer is, the steeper the edges of the CMOS circuitry will be, however also the threshold voltage will be reduced the thinner the gate oxide gets.

\subsubsection{Polysilicon growth}
In this step we need to grow the material for making the gate contact. We use polysilicon instead of metal because polysilicon doesn't melt away during annealing and other high temperature steps.

	\centering

		\input{tikz_process_steps/active.2.b.tex}
	\end{tikzpicture}
	\caption{Polysilicon layer growth}
\end{figure}
The LPCVD polysilicon is deposited at 620\degree C and then doped using $POCl_3$  with 1100 ppm partials pressure at 950\degree C for 30 minutes.
